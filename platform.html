Platform

A smarter operating model for breast imaging

BreastScreening-AI is being developed as a clinician-controlled platform that brings imaging review, AI assistance and explainable interaction into one workflow. The business objective is straightforward: help healthcare providers improve consistency, use specialist time more effectively and introduce AI without removing clinical authority.

Business value

Designed around the pressures facing imaging providers

Breast imaging services must balance growing case complexity, limited specialist capacity, quality expectations and the practical challenge of introducing AI into established clinical systems.

Make specialist time go further

AI-assisted review is designed to reduce avoidable interaction time and support greater operational capacity without removing the radiologist from the decision.

Support diagnostic consistency

A second-reader layer can highlight disagreement and provide structured information for the clinician to review before finalizing an assessment.

Improve adoption across teams

Personalized explanations can support less experienced and senior clinicians differently, helping organizations introduce AI across mixed-experience teams.

Build measurable value

The platform strategy connects technical performance with workflow, decision, usability and trust measures that matter to clinical and operational leaders.

Product capabilities

A platform architecture for assisted breast imaging

The product direction combines four capabilities that can be validated and introduced progressively according to the customer environment and intended use.

Unified image review

A coordinated interface for mammography, ultrasound, MRI and relevant case information, with evidence maturity tracked separately for each modality.

AI second reading

Classification and lesion-localization outputs provide an additional perspective while preserving clinician review and override.

Explainable assistance

Visible findings, contextual arguments and communication adapted to clinician experience support informed rather than automatic reliance.

Workflow measurement

Decision changes, time, usability, workload and interaction can be evaluated alongside model performance.

Traceable decisions

The intended workflow distinguishes the initial clinician assessment, AI recommendation and final clinician decision.

Progressive integration

Technical integration, human factors, clinical validation and economic evaluation are treated as separate deployment gates.

Adoption path

From evidence-backed prototype to deployable product

Healthcare customers need more than an algorithm. Adoption depends on integration, clinical validation, governance, training and a value case grounded in local workflow data.

Current assets

  • Peer-reviewed clinician studies with cohorts of 31, 45 and 52 participants.
  • Research across mammography, ultrasound and MRI.
  • Protected multimodal and clinician-adaptive intellectual property.
  • Project-level TRL 5 progressing toward TRL 6.

Required before scaled adoption

  • Locked intended use and product configuration.
  • Independent multicenter and prospective clinical validation.
  • Quality management, regulatory authorization and post-market controls.
  • Customer-specific budget impact and return-on-investment evidence.

Commercial position

BreastScreening-AI remains research software under progressive product, human-factors and clinical evaluation. Commercial discussions should focus on validation partnerships, integration planning and evidence generation rather than claims of current clinical authorization.
