Purpose

A future home for cross-pilot evidence

The Evidence page will later summarize overall clinical evidence from several pilots and studies. It should separate confirmed facts, exploratory results, peer-reviewed findings, regulatory evidence, and health-economic claims.

Clinical validity

Diagnostic performance, modality-specific evidence, reader agreement, BI-RADS alignment and external validation results.

Workflow impact

Reading allocation, turnaround time, recall burden, workload, usability, trust and adoption evidence.

Economic value

Budget impact, avoided false-positive burden, resource utilization and return-on-validation assumptions.

Evidence pipeline

What this page should eventually consolidate

The structure is intentionally ready for future clinical pilots while avoiding premature claims today.

Exploratory pilots

Early feasibility, usability and workflow findings, clearly marked as preliminary.

Prospective validation

Locked protocols, prespecified endpoints, representative sites and independent review.

Regulatory evidence

Intended use, risk controls, quality management and post-market monitoring requirements.

Health economics

Local cost assumptions, resource use, false-positive burden and budget impact models.

Equity and safety

Subgroup performance, scanner/site variation, accessibility and automation-bias monitoring.

Publication record

Peer-reviewed studies, theses, patents and independently reproducible research outputs.
