Before and after

Different page, different job: this is the operating room of the product story

The platform page explains what the product is and why it matters commercially. The workflow page should show how a site would actually run, evaluate and govern the product in practice.

Current fragmentation

Disconnected review

  1. MG, US and MRI are often reviewed in separate moments.
  2. AI tools, when present, may sit outside the main reporting habit.
  3. Time, disagreement and decision changes are rarely measured consistently.
  4. Procurement teams struggle to connect AI performance to local workflow value.
Target workflow

Measured decision loop

  1. Case context, imaging and AI outputs enter one validation pathway.
  2. The clinician reviews first, then reconciles AI support with judgement.
  3. Every handoff records time, modality, decision status and accountability.
  4. Operational value is tested with site-specific KPIs before scale-up.

Clinician-AI decision loop

The product should interrupt only where a decision can improve

The operational design separates initial judgement, AI assistance, reconciliation and final responsibility. That separation is what makes the workflow measurable and governable.

1

Initial read

Clinician reviews case before seeing AI output when the protocol requires independent baseline measurement.

2

AI signal

System provides classification, localization, explanation or confidence signal according to validated intended use.

3

Disagreement check

Clinician compares findings and identifies whether AI changes attention, certainty or next step.

4

Final decision

Clinician records final assessment; the workflow records timing, change and accountability.

Deployment checklist

What must be true before a workflow pilot starts

This section makes the workflow page actionable for implementation planning rather than repeating the platform's market story.

Clinical protocol

  • Define intended-use boundary.
  • Select eligible cases and modalities.
  • Agree baseline and AI-assisted reading order.
  • Define escalation and review rules.

Data and systems

  • Confirm PACS/RIS or research-environment access.
  • Set de-identification and retention rules.
  • Validate image display and metadata completeness.
  • Log clinician, AI and final decision states.

People and governance

  • Train readers and support staff.
  • Assign safety, IT and clinical owners.
  • Monitor usability, trust and workload.
  • Review pilot results before any scale-up.

Human oversight remains the control point

The workflow may organize multimodal information, provide AI-assisted perspective and measure local value. It does not replace a qualified clinician, institutional protocol, BI-RADS assessment, regulatory obligations, diagnosis or medical advice.
